Michael Jackson1958 - 2009

“Tened piedad porque llevo mucho tiempo sangrando”.

Time line

  • August 29, 1958
    Born in Gary, Indiana (United States), Michael Joseph Jackson was the seventh of the nine children of Joseph and Katherine Jackson. He began his career as just an 11-year-old boy with his brothers on the Jackson Five, with whom he released hit songs like I Want You Back or ABC.
  • 1960
    He began his artistic career in the musical group The Jackson, in which he published, together with some of his brothers, ten albums until 1975.
  • 1971
    Began his career as a soloist, although he continued to belong to the group. In the early 1980s, Jackson became a dominant figure in popular music. His music videos, notably "Beat It," "Billie Jean," and "Thriller," from his 1982 album Thriller, are credited as breaking down racial barriers and transforming the medium into a form of art and promotional tool
  • 1979
    World fame came with his solo career, which began in 1979 with the album Off The Wall, which included hits such as Don't Stop 'Til You Get Enough and Rock With You. His second album, Thriller, from 1982, is the best-selling album in history.
  • 1993
    Jackson secretly married in the Dominican Republic to Lisa Marie Presley, Elvis Presley's only daughter. This link caused public opinion to be divided in two: that of those who saw the union as a publicity stunt to get Michael to be the star of all time; and that of his followers who found in this marriage a beautiful love story that linked the king of pop with the king of rock.
  • 1993
    He was charged with child sexual abuse, but the civil case was settled out of court for an undisclosed amount and no formal charges were filed. In 2005, he was tried and acquitted of new charges of child sexual abuse and various other charges after the jury found him not guilty on all counts.
  • June 25, 2009
    Jackson died of acute propofol and benzodiazepine intoxication after going into cardiac arrest. The Los Angeles County coroner ruled his death a homicide, and his personal physician, Conrad Murray, was found guilty of manslaughter. Jackson's death sparked a general commotion, and the live broadcast of his funeral was viewed by more than 2.5 billion people around the world.
  • 1997
    The singer remarried, this time to nurse Deborah Jeanne Rowe, with whom he had two older children: Michael Joseph Jackson, Jr. (also called "Prince"), and Paris Michael Katherine Jackson. They divorced in 1999, and Rowe awarded Jackson full custody of the children. His third child, Prince Michael Jackson II, was born in 2002 without the artist revealing the identity of the child's mother, although it was said that he had used a surrogate mother.
